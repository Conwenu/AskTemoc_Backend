NOTES:
1	Curriculum Requirements can be fulfilled by other approved courses from accredited institutions of higher education. The courses listed are recommended as the most efficient way to satisfy both Core Curriculum and Major Requirements at UT Dallas.
2	Indicates a prerequisite class to be completed before enrolling for upper-division classes. Specific prerequisites are required for certain upper-division courses. Review requirements for your major and choose accordingly.
3	A required course in the Major that also fulfills a Core Curriculum requirement. Semester credit hours are counted in Core Curriculum. Students must choose a different course for each Core Curriculum category
4	MATH 1325 is required for all JSOM undergraduate students. Students may substitute MATH 2413 or MATH 2417 for MATH 1325. Students may substitute MATH 2414 or MATH 2419 for MATH 1326. Students who complete OPRE 3340 instead of MATH 1326 or MATH 2414 or MATH 2419 will require another course to satisfy the 090 Component Area Option Core Curriculum requirement.
5	To make timely degree progress, students should complete MATH 1325 or its equivalent by the end of their first semester at UT Dallas. Students who will not meet this requirement should contact their academic advisor to discuss their degree timeline.
6	Students may substitute MATH 2333 or MATH 2418 or CS 2305 for OPRE 3333.
7	JSOM first-time-in-college freshmen are required to take BCOM 1300 in their first semester. Transfer students and students new to JSOM are required to take BCOM 3300 in their first semester.
8	Free Electives: 0-3 semester credit hours are required for the B.S. in Information Technology and Systems. Students who complete OPRE 3340 instead of MATH 1326 or MATH 2414 or MATH 2419 will require another 090 Component Area Option Core course to meet the 120 semester credit hour requirement for graduation. Students who complete MATH 1326 or MATH 2414 or MATH 2419 instead of OPRE 3340 will require 3 semester credit hours of free electives to meet the 120 semester credit hour requirement for graduation.
9	Students who have completed CS 1436 with a C or better may replace ITSS 3311 with an additional ITSS upper-division course (excluding ITSS 4301 and ITSS 4V90). Students who have completed CS 1337 with a C or better may replace (ITSS 3312 or ITSS 4381) with an additional ITSS upper-division course (excluding ITSS 4301 and ITSS 4V90).
10	Guided Electives - The BS CIST degree program requires students to focus on a specific concentration to obtain in-depth knowledge in a particular business area.
11	An Internship (Practicum) of at least 160 working hours is required for all JSOM majors. The zero semester-credit-hour internship courses, ITSS 4090 Information Technology and Systems Internship or BA 4090 Management Internship, are recommended as the most efficient way to satisfy this requirement. Students who are employed in full-time jobs may fulfill their internship requirement with their current employer as a special project for registration in BA 4090 with preapproval from their program director.
12	A Community Engagement Experience is required for all JSOM majors in fall 2018 catalog or later. Students may fulfill the requirement with BA 4095, BPS 4396, ENTP 4340, IMS 4335, or MKT 4360. The 0 SCH course BA 4095 is recommended as the most efficient way to satisfy the requirement. Students will be registered for BA 4095 for 0 SCH in the semester they complete 100 service hours.
13	ACCT 4301 or ITSS 4301 may not be used to satisfy BS CIST degree plan requirements.
14	Students must ensure that they have at least 6 semester credit hours of guided electives with an ITSS prefix not including ITSS 4301 and ITSS 4V90.
15	ITSS 4381 can satisfy the ITSS 3312 major preparatory requirement in prior BS CIST catalogs if the course is not used elsewhere on the degree plan.

Be sure to check prerequisites of 2000-level and higher courses.
